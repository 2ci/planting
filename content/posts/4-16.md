Just three days ago, I planted basil seeds, and today they are already showing off. I noticed the tiny "elbows" of the sprouts pushing through the soil, followed quickly by the first set of miniature leaves. 

Normally, the books say to expect germination between five to fourteen days, so seeing this progress in just seventy-two hours feels like a small win. It’s a reminder that whether I’m troubleshooting build failures on AWS Amplify or tending to my garden, success comes down to the right environment and a little patience. Watching these tiny greens emerge so ahead of schedule is the perfect boost for a Thursday afternoon. There is something deeply satisfying about documenting growth, both in my code and in the soil.
